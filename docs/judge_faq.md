# Judge FAQ — Evidence-Backed Answers

## 1. Why is title weight high (0.24)?

The JD asks for a **Senior AI Engineer** founding role. Title weight captures **role evidence** (e.g., "Recommendation Systems Engineer") — not seniority gaming. Plain-language Tier-5 candidates are caught by `career_semantic` + bi-encoder (`semantic_score` 70% embedding blend). We do **not** claim title alone selects winners. **Evidence:** `challenge/jd_config.py`, `DEFAULT_WEIGHTS`, `docs/evaluation_limitations.md`.

## 2. How do plain-language Tier-5 profiles rank with title-heavy weights?

Title is one of 10 weighted signals. Career descriptions feed `career_semantic`, `production_score`, and bi-encoder cosine — a strong plain-language shipper can outrank a weak-titled keyword stuffer. **Evidence:** `challenge/features.py`, `challenge/redrob_ranker.py` component breakdown in reasoning.

## 3. Why do embeddings matter?

Committed `embeddings.fp16.npz` (100K×384, MiniLM-L6-v2@1110a243) blends 70% bi-encoder + 30% lexical. Without `.npz`: top-10 positional overlap drops to 3/10 (set still 10/10). **Evidence:** `reproduce_ranking.sh` no-embeddings check, `data/embeddings/meta.json`.

## 4. Why is the cross-encoder OFF?

CE-on artifacts were not reproducible (3/10 top-10 drift in prior audit). CE needs HF network/cache; spec requires network OFF. Submission: `RANKER_USE_CROSS_ENCODER=0`. **Evidence:** `rerank.py`, `requirements-ranker.txt`, hash `6bf83241…`.

## 5. Why are metrics "proxies"?

No access to hidden GT. Self-consistency proxy is circular (disclosed). Behavioral proxy uses non-ranker signals. Synthetic labels are **rule-generated** — not human. **Evidence:** `docs/evaluation_limitations.md`, `docs/evaluation_honesty_statement.md`.

## 6. Are "hand labels" human?

**No.** `synthetic_proxy_labels.json` is auto-generated by `scripts/build_synthetic_proxy_labels.py`. Labelers: `rule_based_proxy_v1`, `heuristic_relevance_proxy_v1`. Submission top-100 is **excluded** to avoid self-grading.

## 7. Why no hidden GT claims?

Organizer holds secret labels. Expect composite ~0.60–0.68. Offline NDCG numbers are diagnostics only. **Evidence:** `submission_metadata.yaml`, `eval_report.json` note.

## 8. Why offline at rank time?

CE off; numpy + committed `.npz`; Docker `network_mode: none`. **Evidence:** `docker-compose.ranker.yml`, metadata `has_network_during_ranking: false`.

## 9. Why deterministic?

No RNG; ties by `candidate_id`. Recorded hash: `data/SUBMISSION_ARTIFACT.sha256`. **Evidence:** two-run byte identity.

## 10. Cross-machine numpy / score bytes?

Pinned `numpy>=2.3.5,<2.5.0`. If hash differs but top-100 order matches, `verify_submission_artifact.py` PASSes with diagnostic message. **Evidence:** `requirements-ranker.txt`, verify script secondary gate.

## 11. Why is precompute allowed?

One-time off-the-clock embedding job (spec-allowed). Ranking uses committed artifact only. **Evidence:** `docs/precompute_policy.md`, `meta.json elapsed_seconds: 624.9`.

## 12. Why CPU only?

~48s on 100K; fp16 matrix ~71 MB. No GPU in submission path.

## 13. Behavioral twins / honeypots?

`test_behavioral_twin_breaks_tie` proves engagement signals affect ranker. `test_keyword_stuffer_far_from_top` proves keyword stuffing is demoted. Structural honeypots: 0 in top-100. **Evidence:** `challenge/test_ranker.py`.

## 14. NDCG on truly human-labeled set?

We do **not** have one. Stated explicitly in `evaluation_honesty_statement.md`. Synthetic proxy is diagnostic only.

## 15. Scaling to 2M in 5 minutes?

Current: linear scan ~48s/100K → ~8 min/2M on same CPU. Production path: ANN index (FAISS/HNSW), shard by geography, precomputed embeddings — documented as future work; submission scope is 100K offline rank.
